1. What Personal Data We Collect

Under the DPDP Act, 2023, "personal data" refers to any data about an individual who is identifiable by or in relation to such data. We may collect the following types of personal data when you use Chatsphere AI:

  • Personal Information: Your name, email address, or other contact information you provide when registering or interacting with the App, including User IDs or profile details.
  • Usage Data: Information about how you interact with the App, such as activity logs, preferences, and usage patterns, collected with your consent.
  • Device and Technical Data: Device information, including IP address, browser type, operating system, and device identifiers, collected to ensure the App functions properly.
  • Location Data: Approximate location data (e.g., derived from your IP address) if you enable location services, with your explicit consent as required under the DPDP Act.
  • Cookies and Tracking Technologies: We may use cookies, web beacons, or similar technologies to enhance your experience and analyze usage, with your consent where required.

We do not intentionally collect sensitive personal data (e.g., biometric data, financial information, or health data) unless explicitly required for a specific feature and with your explicit consent, as mandated by the DPDP Act.

2. How We Use Your Personal Data

We use your personal data for the following purposes, in compliance with the DPDP Act’s principles of purpose limitation and data minimization:

  • To Provide and Improve the App: To operate, maintain, and enhance Chatsphere AI, including personalizing your experience and developing new features, with your consent or as necessary for the performance of the App.
  • Authentication and Security: To verify your identity, secure your account, and prevent fraud or unauthorized access, as part of our legitimate interests and legal obligations.
  • Communication: To send you updates, notifications, or customer support responses related to the App, with your consent or as necessary for contract performance.
  • Analytics: To analyze usage trends and improve our services, using aggregated and anonymized data where possible, or with your consent for non-aggregated data.
  • Legal Compliance: To comply with the DPDP Act, 2023, other Indian laws, or legal processes (e.g., court orders, regulatory requests).

3. How We Share Your Personal Data

We do not sell your personal data. We may share your data in the following circumstances, in compliance with the DPDP Act:

  • With Service Providers: We may engage third-party vendors (e.g., cloud hosting providers, analytics tools) based in India or abroad to assist with operating the App. These providers are contractually obligated to protect your data and use it only for the purposes we specify, under appropriate safeguards like Standard Contractual Clauses or other mechanisms approved under the DPDP Act for cross-border data transfers.
  • With Your Consent: We may share your data with third parties if you give us explicit consent, as required under the DPDP Act.
  • For Legal Reasons: We may disclose your data if required by law, to protect our rights, or to comply with legal processes (e.g., court orders, subpoenas) under Indian jurisdiction, including those issued by authorities in Bengaluru or Karnataka.
  • Business Transfers: If we are involved in a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your data may be transferred as part of that transaction, with notification and your consent where required by law.

5. Your Rights as a Data Principal

Under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, you, as a data principal, have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to Confirmation and Access: You can request confirmation of whether we process your personal data and access a copy of the data we hold about you.
  • Right to Correction: You can ask us to update or correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten): You can request the deletion of your personal data, subject to legal obligations or other exceptions under the DPDP Act.
  • Right to Data Portability: You can request a copy of your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: If we process your data based on consent, you can withdraw it at any time, though this may affect your ability to use certain features of the App.
  • Right to Grievance Redressal: You can raise concerns or complaints about our data practices, and we will address them promptly.
